NATIONAL ANIMAL OF INDIA

Bengal Tiger

Bengal tigers are mainly found in India, Bangladesh, Nepal, and Bhutan. They have a bright orange coat with black stripes and a white underside. They are strong swimmers and often cool off in rivers and lakes. Bengal tigers are the most common subspecies, but still endangered. Their major threats are poaching and habitat destruction.

South-China Tiger

The South China tiger is one of the rarest tiger subspecies in the world. It is possibly extinct in the wild, with only a few in captivity. These tigers are smaller and have shorter fur than other tigers. They were once found in central and southern China. Conservationists are trying to reintroduce them into the wild.

Malayan Tiger

Malayan tigers are found in the tropical rainforests of the Malay Peninsula. They are smaller and more agile compared to other tigers. They have a similar appearance to Bengal tigers but are genetically distinct. Their population is very low, with fewer than 150 left in the wild. Habitat loss and poaching are major threats to their survival

Siberian Tiger

Siberian tigers live in the snowy forests of eastern Russia and northeast China. They are the largest and heaviest tiger subspecies. Their thick fur and large paws help them survive in cold climates. They are solitary and need large territories to hunt. Conservation efforts have helped increase their wild population
